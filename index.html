Janet Wort

Who is Janet Wort?
I take pride in my commitment, honesty, integrity, and transparency. The main strengths I bring to the council table are my financial knowledge, as well as my administrative and organizational skills. I try to be a good listener.
  • moved to Hope in 1999, from Kamloops
  • am married to Ron Wort, who has lived here since 1954
  • have varied work experience in banking, insurance and finance, 14 years with the
    • Federal government.
Why am I running for Council?
I am semi-retired and have chosen to run as a Councillor to bring some changes to our Town. Our community has much to offer, but I have seen areas that need improvement. We can make Hope more of a tourist destination. The natural beauty of our surroundings speaks for itself.
My Priorities
  • road and water service improvement
  • developing affordable housing including addressing homelessness (which includes holding the Province accountable for providing services in our community)
  • no development in Memorial Park
  • minimal increase in property taxes
My community involvement includes:
  • founding member and current President of the Hope & District Arts Council
  • co-chair of the Coalition for the Preservation of the Hope Station House
  • part of the group that is supporting the extension of the Mount Hope lookout trail.

Dave Fernie

Who is Dave Fernie?
I have been a summer resident of Hope since 1996, and in 2011 invested in property and built our home in 2012, becoming a permanent resident on Kawkawa Lake Rd. I am Married to Lianne, with 2 adult children, I am a retired owner of a telecom business and prior to that started out in Jasper, Alberta as a CN Locomotive Engineer for 13 Years and moving to CN Vancouver during that time.

Arlene Webster

Why am I running for public office?
The answer is that we need change and this past year, fighting to save the Hope Station House as Chairperson of the Coalition to Preserve the Hope Station House, has shown me how much I love our community and how hard I am willing to work to preserve our history and our small town way of life.
Government needs to be transparent with no hidden agenda.
We all deserve our elected officials to be honest, open, ready to listen to the electorate and work diligently for the best interests of our community. I am committed to those values.
I am not new to the political arena.
Councillor for 6 years and School Trustee for 9 years. If I am elected, I will bring that experience and knowledge to the Council table.
I have lived almost all of my life in Hope.
I have seen Hope go from a resource and tourism based community to a community working to find a new identity in a fast paced world. Our community has much to offer; but, we have to continue to highlight and develop its assets if we want to find that special niche where it will shine.
I have over 40 years of experience as a business woman and volunteer.
Currently, I am working on the Mt. Hope Lookout trail to extend it to the upper bluff, an exciting undertaking for our community and a stunning addition to our trail system.
Where do I see room for improvement in our community?
  • Repair our roads and streets
  • Address homelessness and hold the provincial government accountable for ensuring appropriate services are provided for the individuals they wish to house in our community
  • Keep tax increases at a minimum.
  • Protect Memorial Park - it's a gem in the midst of our town, and it is to Hope what Stanley Park is to Vancouver.
  • Reduce crime
  • Provide sustainable employment and incentives to attract new businesses
  • Build more affordable housing
  • Improve communication and collaboration with minority communities
  • Rexamine snow removal
